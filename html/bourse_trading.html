Brief introduction of bitcoin

The concept of BitCoin was originally proposed by Zhong Ben Cong in 2009, based on the design of open source software and the construction of its P2P network based on the idea of ccong. Bitcoin is a digital currency in the form of P2P. Point to point transmission means a decentralized payment system. Unlike most currencies, bitcoin is not issued by a specific monetary institution. It is generated by a large number of calculations based on a specific algorithm. The bitcoin uses a distributed database made up of many nodes in the P2P network to confirm and record all transactions, and uses cryptographic design to ensure the circulation of money. Security of the link. The de centralization of P2P and the algorithm itself ensure that no currency can be manipulated manually by making a large number of bitcoins. Cryptography based design enables bitcoins to be transferred or paid by real owners only. This also ensures the anonymity of currency ownership and circulation transactions. The biggest difference between bitcoin and other virtual currencies is that its total quantity is very limited and has a very strong scarcity. The monetary system had no more than 10 million 500 thousand in 4 years, and the total number would be permanently limited to 21 million.
